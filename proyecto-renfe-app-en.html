Renfe App Redesign

Improving the user experience in rail transport through user-centered research, wireframing and interactive prototyping

Figma UX research User experience User interface Wireframing Prototiping User testing

Context

The problem

The current Renfe application has limitations in usability and inclusivity. Users face friction in basic processes such as ticket purchasing, schedule checking, and incident management. The navigation lacks intuitiveness and the information architecture is not optimized for travelers' actual needs.

The objectives

Create a user experience that simplifies existing processes, improves accessibility for all audiences, and optimizes the efficiency of the rail service. The guiding question was: What improvements foster ease of use, inclusivity, sustainability, and service efficiency?

The audience

A broad and diverse audience: frequent travelers who prioritize efficiency and speed, and occasional users who seek simplicity and clarity. Special attention to inclusivity for people with different levels of digital literacy and abilities.

Approach

Research conducted

The process was structured around three methodologies: digital immersion in similar apps from countries with higher rail standards (HSL from Finland, NS from the Netherlands, SBB Mobile from Switzerland), identifying best practices in QR codes and multimodal integration. Empirical research as a user of the current app, documenting friction points and opportunities. Qualitative interviews with three differentiated profiles that informed the creation of User Personas and User Journeys, synthesized through Affinity Diagrams.

Design decisions

The architecture was simplified into four categories: Trip Planner (integrating purchasing, schedules, and incidents), My Tickets (QR, PDF, history, and favorites), Profile (personal data and Renfe points), and Settings & Help (personalized alerts, chat, FAQ, and legal documentation). Card Sorting with users validated that fewer categories with clear functions significantly improves navigability.

Technical challenges

Simplifying without losing functionality required multiple wireframing iterations. Intuitive naming presented challenges, with terms like "Trip Planner" demanding semantic adjustments. The contextual alert system prioritizes relevant incidents with customizable options. Digital accessibility was a priority, considering touch sizes, contrast, and readability in critical flows.

Tech Stack

Figma

Main tool for wireframing, interface design, and creation of the high-fidelity interactive prototype with over 30 connected screens.

User Research

Qualitative research methodologies: semi-structured interviews, empirical immersion, competitive analysis, and synthesis through Affinity Diagrams.

UX Design

Creation of User Personas, User Journeys, use scenarios, information architecture, Card Sorting, and thoroughly documented interaction flows.

Usability Testing

Evaluation with real users through specific tasks, post-test questionnaires, and findings analysis for design iteration.
